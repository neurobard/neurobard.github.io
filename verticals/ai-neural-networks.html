AI & Neural Networks

Brain-inspired architectures, neuromorphic computing, computational neuroscience, and the convergence of artificial and biological intelligence.

Where Silicon Meets Synapse

The relationship between neuroscience and artificial intelligence has always been bidirectional. Neural networks were originally inspired by biological neurons, and today, the most advanced AI architectures are increasingly drawing on insights from neuroscience to solve fundamental challenges in learning, memory, and reasoning.

Large language models have revealed unexpected structural parallels with cortical processing. Research published in Nature Neuroscience demonstrates that transformer attention mechanisms mirror aspects of how the brain allocates cognitive resources across competing information streams. This convergence is not coincidental — it suggests that both biological and artificial systems face similar computational constraints.

Neuromorphic Computing

Neuromorphic chips, designed to emulate the brain's energy-efficient event-driven processing, are achieving 100x improvements in energy efficiency over traditional architectures for certain workloads. Intel's Loihi 2, IBM's NorthPole, and a wave of startup neuromorphic processors are finding applications in edge computing, autonomous vehicles, and always-on sensing.

AI-Powered Neural Decoding

Perhaps the most exciting convergence is in neural signal decoding. AI models trained on brain activity can now translate cortical signals into coherent speech, reconstruct visual scenes from fMRI data, and predict motor intentions with millisecond precision. These capabilities are the foundation for next-generation brain-computer interfaces.
