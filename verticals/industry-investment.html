The $53 Billion Opportunity

The global neurotechnology market was valued at approximately $15-19 billion in 2024-2025 and is projected to reach $53-67 billion by 2034-2035, growing at 13-14% CAGR. This growth is being driven by an expanding clinical pipeline, surging consumer adoption, government research initiatives, and increasing AI integration.

Investment in neurotechnology companies has climbed from roughly €582 million in 2022 to €2 billion in 2024, with forecasts reaching €3.5 billion by 2025. Notably, fewer new companies are forming while existing companies are raising larger rounds — a signal of market maturation and consolidation around the most promising technologies.

Key Segments

Neurostimulation dominates with approximately 46% market share, driven by established DBS and spinal cord stimulation platforms. Brain-computer interfaces, though smaller at roughly $500M, represent the fastest-growing segment at 16.5% CAGR. Enabling software — analytics, closed-loop algorithms, and AI-powered signal processing — is an underappreciated $4B segment that underpins the entire ecosystem.

What Smart Money Is Watching

Investors are increasingly focused on companies at the intersection of AI and neurotechnology. Neuralink's $500M raise at an $8.5B valuation, Paradromics' NEOM-backed investment, and INBRAIN Neuroelectronics' $50M Series B for graphene-based BCIs signal where the capital is flowing. The convergence of consumer wellness and clinical therapeutics creates dual-market companies that can monetize across segments.
